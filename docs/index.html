What’s cookin’?

It does the counting so you don’t have to.

No calorie app, no weighing food, no forms. You just say what you ate in plain words — it works out the numbers, keeps the tally, and uses your history to pick what’s next.

01

Works out the calories for you

Describe a meal in plain words; it estimates the calories from typical Singapore hawker portions. Know the number? Say it and it uses yours instead — or “make it 700” to fix its guess.

The secret formula

There’s no secret formula. The AI just turns text into text — everything useful is plain plumbing around it.

(Sorry, Plankton.)

The idea

Nothing clever hides inside the model. A weekday timer fires at 11:50, small tools log a meal or search for food, a chat talks to you, a dashboard shows the tally. Swap the model for another and it all still works — it’s one small program, readable end to end.

Where it’s headed

Toward more context, not more surface. A better sense of your week and your favourites, smarter variety in the picks, the occasional summary — while staying small, with no database and no account. New abilities arrive as little tools, never as a bigger app to maintain.

Not on the menu

Kitchen’s closed for these.

The restraint is the point. These are choices, not missing features — leaving them out is what keeps it light enough to actually use.

🦀 Not on the menu by design, not by accident
  • ✕
    Apps & accounts — no install, no login, no onboarding. It’s a chat you already have.
  • ✕
    Gram-perfect macro tracking — it rounds to a sensible number you can fix in one reply. Precision theatre, skipped.
  • ✕
    Guilt & lectures — one gentle nudge when you’re running high, never shaming or moralising about food.
  • ✕
    Medical or diet advice — it’s a lunch helper, not a nutritionist or a health authority.
  • ✕
    Nagging & notifications — it replies when you write, and suggests once a day. That’s the whole cadence.
  • ✕
    Servers & databases to run — it runs on a schedule and a chat, with nothing to provision or keep alive.
