What is this?

A lunch assistant that fits inside a chat thread.

No dashboard to open before you eat, no forms to fill. You text it in plain words; it does three things and stays out of the way.

01 / LOG

Say what you ate

“Laksa and a teh.” It estimates the calories, logs the meal, and tells you the running total for the day. Give your own number and it uses that.

02 / CORRECT

Fix it in one reply

“Make it 700.” “Remove that.” “Two plates, actually.” It adjusts and confirms the new total — no menus, no editing screens.

03 / SUGGEST

Get one pick at 11:50

Every weekday it reads your recent meals, searches eateries near the office, and suggests a single spot that fits the calories you have left.

How it works

Two paths. No server to babysit.

Chatting happens the instant you text. The daily pick runs on a schedule. Both write their state back to a Git repo, which redraws the dashboard.

Who it’s for

One person, one office, one daily question.

This isn’t a platform for everyone. It’s tuned to a single person who works at 1 Depot Rd and eats lunch nearby — and it kills the small, daily decision that costs more energy than it should.

Why it’s built this way

The model is just a text-in, text-out function. The harness — the loop, the tools, the interface — is the product.

The idea

Everything useful lives around the model, not inside it: the schedule that fires at 11:50, the tools that log a meal or search for food, the chat that talks to you, the dashboard that shows the tally. Swap the model out and the harness still stands. It’s one Python file — the whole loop, readable end to end.

Where it’s headed

Toward more context, not more surface. Better sense of your week and your favourites, smarter variety in the picks, the occasional summary — while staying one file with no database and no account. New abilities arrive as small tools bolted onto the harness, never as a bigger app to maintain.

What it deliberately doesn’t do

Not on the menu.

The restraint is the point. These are choices, not missing features — leaving them out is what keeps it light enough to actually use.

Kitchen’s closed for — by design, not by accident
  • ✕
    Apps & accounts — no install, no login, no onboarding. It’s a chat you already have.
  • ✕
    Gram-perfect macro tracking — it rounds to a sensible number you can fix in one reply. Precision theatre, skipped.
  • ✕
    Guilt & lectures — one gentle nudge when you’re running high, never shaming or moralising about food.
  • ✕
    Medical or diet advice — it’s a lunch helper, not a nutritionist or a health authority.
  • ✕
    Nagging & notifications — it replies when you write, and suggests once a day. That’s the whole cadence.
  • ✕
    Servers & databases to run — state is a JSON file in a Git repo; there’s nothing to provision or keep alive.
